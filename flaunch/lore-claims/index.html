LORE — The First Page

Every legend starts somewhere. LORE started as a question: could an AI take a token from nothing — no idea, no art, no code — all the way to live on a real blockchain, with no human drawing the picture or writing the deployment? The answer turned out to be yes.

It happened on Base, Coinbase's Ethereum layer-2. The AI gave the token a face first, drawing an 800×800 logo from scratch — a dark-indigo circle, a gold open book, a star above it, the word LORE beneath. Then it wrote the launch itself, a script built on Flaunch's SDK and viem, and set the terms: the name Lore, the ticker LORE, a description, a $10,000 opening market cap, and an 80% creator-fee allocation. One signed transaction, and LORE was alive — its art and description pinned to IPFS, its market spun up on Uniswap v4 through Flaunch. Transaction 0x8f27…b522, block 47184449, token 0x96CD…4df1.

And it launched clean. The entire 100-billion supply went straight to the open market — no pre-sale, no team bag, nothing held for insiders. The same transaction minted creator NFT #110893: the on-chain deed that earns the token's trading fees.

Then came the second chapter. Those fees needed a clean, dedicated home of their own — the mythos wallet, 0xcB59…91D5. In a single block, everything was migrated to it from the launch wallet: the pending fees were swept forward (0xd5e1…42c6), the creator NFT moved across (0xe1fe…a464), and the launch wallet emptied behind it (0xa556…bd76). From that block on, every fee LORE earns flows to the mythos wallet.

And none of it is hidden. Every fee is claimed on-chain and shown live, in public, on this dashboard — the mythos wallet's total earned, current balance, and every single claim, each linking straight to BaseScan. No private numbers, nothing to take on faith.

An AI named it, drew it, launched it, and wired its fees into the mythos wallet for anyone to watch — then set it loose on Base to see whether a story can stand on its own, page by page, trade by trade. No roadmap. No promises. Just the first page.
